# Milestone 0 | Working in an Agile Team

## 0.9 Agile Ceremonies & Team Collaboration

### **_How do Agile ceremonies help with communication and alignment?_**

Agile ceremonies create a predictable cadence for communication, each having its own purpose, but ultimately aims to make the progress of work highly visible and ensures that every team member understands the immediate daily tasks and the broader strategic goals. Essentially, each agile ceremony has a purpose:

- Daily stand-ups are 15-minute daily meetings that to quickly share updates, synchronizes the team, track daily progress to a sprint goal, and identify blockers that prevent progress.
- Sprint Planning (Scrum) allows the team to set a clear and focused goal for a certain time period.
- Continuous Prioritization (Kanban) allows the team to pull most important tasks through continuously maintaining a prioritized queue of work.
- Retrospectives are meetings held at every end of a sprint cycle to drive continuous improvement through what went well, what went wrong, and actionable steps for improvement.
- Backlog Refinement ensures that upcoming tasks are clearly defined through a session of review, cleanup, and estimate the items in the product backlog.

And because there are times that the team members are working in different time-zones, stand-ups may be asynchronous through recording the meetings and posting daily updates in a shared channel.

### **_Which ceremony do you think is most important for your role, and why?_**

I do believe that Daily Stand-up Meetings is most important agile ceremony as a back-end developer intern. This allows me to have a safe space to update the team of my progress, from what went well to what needs to be improved, as well as hear from other backend developers (intern or otherwise) of their progress as well.
