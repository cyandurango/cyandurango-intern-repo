# Milestone 0 | Working in an Agile Team

## 0.12 Working in an Agile Team

Goal: Understand how Focus Bear’s agile team operates, including roles, workflows, and collaboration practices.

### **_What do you think are the biggest benefits and challenges of Agile?_**

The Agile process is largely beneficial to today;s time due to its high adaptability to market changes and allows for faster delivery of usable features. It allows for continuous feedback loops while maintaining tight team alignment.

It's main challenge is the immense discipline needed to define boundaries that lays the groundwork in enabling better workflow. If these boundaries are undefined, the Agile process can easily devolve into a "scope creep" where endless changes are being made without a clearly-defined goal.

### **_How can Agile principles help improve your role and responsibilities?_**

The four principles of Agile are: (1) Individuals and interactions over process and tools, (2) Working software over comprehensive documentation, (3) Customer collaboration over contract negotiation, and (4) Responding to change over following a plan. These principles are what makes the whole project breakable into manageable tickets.

For this reason, Kanban is what is being used in Focus Bear because Kanban's flexibility aligns with the principles of Agile. Scrum on the other hand is too rigid and too predictable, demanding large and forward-looking planning sessions and weeks-long commitments that may be too overwhelming. Kanban's features are not only great for continuous maintenance, its capability to visualize tasks greatly helps neurodivergent developers as well. Through its Work-in-Progress Limits, it enforces single-tasking, reduces cognitive load, minimizes context switching, and provides immediate visual feedback.
