# Milestone 0 | Working in an Agile Team

## 0.11 Roles in an Agile Team

### **_What is your role, and how does it contribute to the Agile process?_**

My role in the agile team is a "Developer", specifically a back-end developer, developing the architecture of the back-end side of the application/service that is currently being developed.

My role contributes to the Agile process through delivering value by developing functional pieces of software that breaks down database architectures and complex API routes into testable tasks, providing technical reality checks to ensure that the tasks are feasible in the given time frame, and pipelining through continuous integration/continuous deployment allowing th team to merge code multiple times a day without breaking the application in itself

### **_How do your responsibilities overlap or interact with other roles in the team?_**

My responsibilities as a back-end developer cascade into the other agile roles, most notably toward the front-end developers as the data structure must be agreed between both to ensure that the application's functions would work seamlessly with the front-end's design. As for the other roles:

- with Quality Assurance, collaboration is tight and fast as the QA is responsible for testing the outputs of the developers.
- with Product Managers, they define what the business needs, and it is the developer's job as to how to build it securely and efficiently,
- with Agile Project Managers, all technical blockers or on-hold tasks and overall progress of the tasks must be communicated, relying on honest updates to keep on track.
