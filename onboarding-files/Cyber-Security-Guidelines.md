## 0.7 Cyber Security

Goal: Understand how to keep company and user data secure by following cyber security best practices.

## RESEARCH AND LEARN

### **_What are common cyber security threats in a remote work environment?_**

Common cybersecurity threats include unsecured Wi-Fi in using public or home networks without proper encryption, phishing emails or messages, data leaks due to shadow IT, and physical security from family or visitor devices.

### **_What are best practices for keeping your devices and accounts secure?_**

- Enable auto-updates for the software to keep the device software updated, allowing vulnerability patching.
- Utilize VPNs when connecting through public networks
- Implement a zero-trust architecture that assumes every device is unsafe and needs verification upon connection.

### **_Why is it important to lock your computer when away from your desk?_**

This is the basics of security that reduces the risk of unauthorized physical access of my computer from any and all factors that threaten its security.

### **_How should you handle phishing attempts and suspicious links?_**

- Inspect through hovering over the links to see the actual URL.
- Check misspellings of sender names
- Report suspected phishing links and emails.

### **_What makes a strong password, and why should you use a password manager?_**

Strong passwords are typically made up of a string of 12+ characters of different types (alphabetic, numeric, or miscellaneous characters). Password managers help their users store passwords from different accounts and are stored.

### **_Why is two-factor authentication (2FA) important, and when should you enable it?_**

2FA adds a second layer of defense that needs verification from a trusted device. This prevents hackers from readily accessing the account through password only. This means it is essential in having this feature implemented in every scenario.
