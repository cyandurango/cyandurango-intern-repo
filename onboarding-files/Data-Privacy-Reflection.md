# Milestone 0 | Company Policies

## 0.2 Data Privacy & Confidentiality

Goal: Understand how to handle sensitive data responsibly and follow Focus Bear’s privacy and confidentiality policies.

## Research and Learn

### **_Review Focus Bear’s privacy policy and note key takeaways._**

Here are some of the key takeaways I have learned from reading the privacy policy:

- They utilize trusted third-party service providers that operate under strict data privacy laws
- They have outlined repeatedly that the data they collected is processed for legal basis, ensuring transparency toward their users.
- They implement data minimization (collecting only the needed data), anonymizing data, prompt deletion, masking data, and other security measures to ensure collected data is protected.

### **_What types of data are considered confidential at Focus Bear?_**

Several personal data are being kept confidentially and are anonymized, encrypted, or handled by third-party services.

- Anonymized data: Occupation data, technical data, profile data, aggregate data
- Encrypted data: Special categories (Religious data), Social data,
- Handled through 3rd-party Services: Identification data, Finance-related data.

### **_What are best practices for handling confidential data?_**

Best practices include utilizing double encryption for sensitive user inputs, anonymizing and aggregating data before analysis, enforcing strict role-based access controls, and delegating highly sensitive data to compliant, trusted third-party services

### **_How should you respond to a suspected data breach or accidental disclosure of confidential information?_**

Generally, the breach must be contained so that it doesn't spread to the rest of the accounts, Assess the scope of the exposed data, Notify the internal security officers and/or legal authorities, and Review the systems and apply necessary firewalls to prevent the breach from happening again.

## Reflection

### **_What steps can you take to ensure you handle data securely in your daily tasks?_**

Practice data minimization, or only ask the data that is only needed for the application. I would ensure utilizing company-approved secure channels when communicating sensitive information

### **_How should you store, share, and dispose of sensitive information safely?_**

Data should be stored as cipher-text, to prevent data from being easily readable. Data and information to be shared should always be of a need-to-know basis. Disposing these information should adhere to strict data retention policies that securely delete or wipe these files.

### **_What are some common mistakes that lead to data privacy issues, and how can they be avoided?_**

A common mistake is accidentally emailing sensitive documents to the wrong recipient, which can be avoided by double-checking the recipient lists and attachments before sending. Another is using weak passwords across any platform, which can be avoided through utilizing a secure password manager as well as enforcing a 2FA service across accounts.
