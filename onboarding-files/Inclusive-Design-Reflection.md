## 0.4 Working with Vulnerable Populations

Goal: Understand how to interact respectfully and responsibly when working with or designing for vulnerable populations, including neurodivergent individuals.

## Research & Learn

### **_Who are considered vulnerable populations, and what challenges might they face in digital spaces?_**

Vulnerable populations include but are not limited to older adults, individuals with disabilities, marginalized ethnic and/or linguistic groups, and those from economically challenged or rural areas. As digital spaces continue to flourish at this world, these people constantly experience the digital divide; they lack the access to high-speed internet and/or modern devices due to affordability issues or the inaccessibility of the digital infrastructure itself. There are also barriers in terms of digital literacy, evident in the lack of confidence in using new technologies, and in the increased risk of exclusion and exploitation when platforms aren't designed with their specific needs.

### **_What ethical considerations are important when designing or working with neurodivergent individuals?_**

There are several, but I can name three: (1) Designing the UX elements that doesn't involve auto-playing videos, aggressive pop-ups, or chaotic color schemes; (2) Allow users to easily do processes without penalty that protects their behavioral and health data meticulously; and (3) Communication must be literal and unambiguous.

### **_How can you make interactions and content more accessible for people with ADHD or Autism?_**

The UX design should be shifted from a purely-aesthetic into a highly-functional, low-friction design. Only necessary information must be shown for an immediate task, with an option to dig deeper should they choose to. Every action must also be easily undone through "undo buttons' and 'multi-click processes'. Robust settings must also be included, such as disabling animations, switching to dark mode, and muting notifications. And lastly, interfaces should be literal rather than metaphorical.

### **_How can we support neurodivergent team members in a professional setting?_**

Through building an environment where their neurodivergent traits are respected more. This entails communicating with daily operational empathy, such as using asynchronous communication allowing them to respond in their own time, and clearly define tasks entailing specific steps and a clear deadline.

## Reflection

### **_How can you adjust your communication style to be more inclusive of neurodivergent users and teammates?_**

By removing the burden of translation from them. This entails starting emails and messages with the exact purpose before proceeding with the context or details, add on several possible ways for people to process information, and assume good intent when making feedback.

### **_What are some common UX or communication pitfalls that might make Focus Bear less accessible or supportive?_**

For me, Focus Bear may become a strict productivity tool rather than a helper application for neurodivergent people if not managed properly. Should the user accidentally miss a habit or break a streak, aggressive UX design may trigger Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria that will make them abandon the application entirely. The user may be forced to configure rules, setup block-lists, and define routines that may not be valuable for them to begin with.

### **_What is one practical change you can make in your work to better support vulnerable populations?_**

I think a good implementation is to allow myself to question the output of the project, document, or design based on clear navigation and unambiguous communication before finalizing them. Through this, I am able to integrate within my workflow their needs and redo the output should any feature be unsuitable for their needs.

I found one in [Reddit](https://www.reddit.com/r/AutisticWithADHD/comments/1qbr4vh/what_productivity_tools_or_apps_actually_work_for/) that talks about the experience of neurodivergent people using typical productivity tools. They said that these productivity apps are built around pressure, streaks, and rigid routines which often backfires.

### **_Identify one design or communication improvement that could make Focus Bear more accessible._**

Focus Bear helps in staying on task through their distraction blocking, but the main challenge is its rigidity. The schedule is not flexible enough for some users, and the set-up can feel confusing. Having users the choice to start their routine whenever they want to will allow them to have autonomy over the application, rather than the app enforcing its rules unto the users.

### **_Practice writing a clear, patient, and supportive response to a hypothetical user struggling with executive functioning._**

"Hi <name_of_user>, thank you for reaching out. I understand the overwhelming feeling, and its simply because your brain is too overloaded. This is very normal and is not a sign of failure.

What you can do is to tackle each task or each routine one at a time. Focus on one task rather than all pending goals. And once that task is done, proceed to the next one. You won't notice it at first, but eventually you will be done with all of your pending tasks. Just take it one at a time.

And if it still feels too much, take a break. You don't have to force yourself when you can't do it. These tasks and routines will always be waiting for you whenever you are ready."
