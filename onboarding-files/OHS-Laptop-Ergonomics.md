## 0.1 Occupational Health & Safety (OHS) for Desk-Based Work

Goal: Understand how to set up a safe and ergonomic workspace, especially when using a laptop, and develop habits to prevent strain and discomfort.

## Research & Learn

### **_What are the risks of using a laptop without an external monitor or keyboard?_**

Using the laptop as is can lead to several physical strains such as: Cervical Kyphosis (pressure on the neck and upper spine), Shoulder and back pain due to hunching forward, Repetitive strain injuries from unnatural hand positioning in the keyboard or mouse, and Computer Vision Syndrome from small screen laptops that lead to squinting and increased eye fatigue.

### **_What ergonomic equipment can improve posture when working on a laptop? (e.g., laptop stands, external keyboards, ergonomic chairs)_**

Ergonomic chairs support and maintain the natural S-curve of the spine. An elevated laptop stand levels the screen to eye level. External keyboards fixes the elbows at a 90-degree angle.

There are also ergonomic mouses designed to rest the hands comfortably, reducing the risk of carpal tunnel syndrome.

### **_What adjustments should be made to monitor height, chair position, and desk setup for a healthier workspace?_**

The golden rule for adjusting the monitor/screen height is that the top third of the screen is at eye level. The chair should be positioned such that the distance between myself and the screen is at arm's length.

### **_What are some daily habits that reduce the impact of prolonged laptop use? (e.g., movement breaks, posture checks, eye strain reduction techniques)_**

I have heard of the 20-20-20 rule, wherein every 20 minutes, look at an object 20 feet away for at least 20 seconds.
