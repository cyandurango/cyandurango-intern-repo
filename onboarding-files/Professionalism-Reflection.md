# Milestone 0 | Company Policies

## 0.3 Professionalism at Focus Bear

Goal: Understand professional expectations at Focus Bear and how to communicate effectively while maintaining a positive and inclusive work environment.

## Research & Learn

### **_What are examples of professional vs. unprofessional behavior in a casual work environment?_**

Professional behavior include but not limited to utilizing the right channels for the right message, arriving on time or earlier during meetings, and offer help as a team member. Unprofessional behavior would include ignoring messages, multitasking during a meeting, or blaming other team members for project fails.

### **_What does respectful communication look like in remote teams?_**

The tone in messaging is what sets the intention. Using a polite greeting and a clear structure would help convey a collaborative tone. Additionally, messages must be re-read to avoid unintended harshness.

### **_How can you give and receive feedback professionally?_**

Feedback must always be focused on the action, rather than the person committing the action. The feedback must be framed such that the situation, behavior, and impact cause by said behavior is highlighted.

## Reflection

### **_Have you ever experienced or witnessed great teamwork and professionalism? What made it effective?_**

Yeah, I have witnessed it. Its effectiveness is only defined by the team's transparency of the progress, problems and solutions to those said problems. It has encouraged more collaboration between team members and is shown through their outputs.

### **_What steps can you take to make sure your communication is clear, respectful, and inclusive?_**

I should start with the purpose of the message when communicating my intent. The terms to use must be clear, accessible, and easy to understand. Listening actively would also help in understanding more of the topic and reply much more appropriately

### **_How can you help create a positive, fun, and professional work environment?_**

Respecting colleague boundaries would keep it professional, but it is also important to celebrate small wins with them as well as be solution-oriented when it comes to solving problems. That way, the team can enjoy each other's company.

### **_How can you prepare effectively for meetings with supervisors and colleagues?_**

The agenda is what summarizes the meeting and is essential to ensure a good flow between all team members. Reviewing the agenda would assure that all are looking forward the same goal. Updates on any type of progress should include a summary of what has been done, what is currently being done, and what is still there to do. Be prepared to answer questions as well, and always have a way to take notes.

### **_What would it look like to be proactive during your internship?_**

Asking for feedback is a sign of proactive-ness, because it shows that I as an intern is working on the project and that the rest of the team members know that I am doing something as well, serving as an update.

### **_How might you follow up with colleagues or supervisors if you need information/action from them? How would you escalate it further if you don't get a response and when would you escalate?_**

Following up would entail balancing persistence with professionalism. Waiting for an appropriate amount of time before replying to a non-urgent matter, and escalate only when the lack of response threatens a critical deadline. The follow-up must also be strictly focused on work-related matters as well.

Somethings I wont do include:

- hiding my mistakes as covering them may threaten the project.
- saying yes to every deadline rather than compromising, increasing the probability of flawed work
- always assume without clarification, leading to guesswork and reduced project quality
