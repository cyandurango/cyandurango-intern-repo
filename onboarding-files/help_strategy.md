# Milestone 10 | Debugging

## 10.1 When you get stuck - what next?

Goal: Develop a strategy to determine when to use search engines, AI tools, or seek help from colleagues during problem-solving.

### **_When do you prefer using AI vs. searching Google?_**

I prefer utilizing AI as a guide to doing code rather than having it do the code for me. It is good at structuring, rubber duck debugging, syntax formatting, and writing tests and documentation. These tasks are usually too chore-like or time-consuming, and letting AI do these tasks would lessen the workload on me and allow me to work on a specific coding block on a certain project. AI can also help me summarize complex topics into digestible and understandable analogies.

This is where searching Google comes in. I prefer searching through Google when going unto a deep dive on a certain function. This is to further understand its use and purpose on the application, that AI just summarizes in two to three sentences. Searching through Google would allow me to make insights and learnings on my own.

### **_How do you decide when to ask a colleague instead?_**

Asking a colleague is essential when it comes to expert opinion and company secrets, as only they can provide insights and understanding to these certain topics that neither AI tools nor Google searches could. In addition, colleagues would allow a fresh set of eyes to look at a certain problem you are stuck with and help you through their insights.

### **_What challenges do developers face when troubleshooting alone?_**

The main challenge is the 'tunnel vision' where developers get hyper-focused in solving what seems to be a complex problem, that in the end was because of a missing or misnamed variable. These type of problems, and others like cognitive overload or lack of line-by-line logic explanation, can easily be reduced if the developer themselves would ask for help, from colleagues, search engines or AI tools.
